<p><p>The automotive alloy wheel market consists of three primary types: casting, forging, and other processes. Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold to create intricate designs, leading to cost-effective production for high-volume needs. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping the metal under high pressure, resulting in wheels that are typically stronger and lighter. Other processes may include machining or a combination of methods, catering to specialized applications and custom designs. Each type offers distinct advantages in terms of performance and aesthetic appeal.</p></p>
