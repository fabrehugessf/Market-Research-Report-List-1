<p><p>The high-temperature stainless steel market comprises two main types: austenitic and ferritic grades. High-temperature austenitic grades, known for their excellent corrosion resistance and mechanical properties, are designed to withstand extreme heat and demanding environments, making them ideal for applications in aerospace, chemical, and power industries. In contrast, high-temperature ferritic grades, generally more cost-effective, offer good oxidation resistance and stability at elevated temperatures, primarily used in industrial and automotive applications. Both types cater to specific performance needs in high-temperature settings.</p></p>
