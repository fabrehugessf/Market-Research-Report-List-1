<p><p>Bioabsorbable Interference Screws are surgical devices made from materials that naturally break down in the body over time. Primarily used in orthopedic procedures, particularly in ligament reconstruction surgeries, these screws provide temporary support for tendon or ligament grafts, facilitating healing while eliminating the need for a second surgery to remove metallic implants. Their biocompatibility and ability to reduce post-operative complications have fostered their increasing adoption in the medical field.</p><p>The Bioabsorbable Interference Screws Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.2% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by advancements in material science leading to the development of more effective and safer bioabsorbable materials, along with the rising incidence of sports-related injuries and advancements in minimally invasive surgeries. Additionally, there is a growing demand for innovative orthopedic solutions that enhance patient recovery times and outcomes. Key trends include the introduction of novel designs and technologies that improve screw fixation strength and reduce the risk of complications. The expansion of healthcare infrastructure and increasing awareness about orthopedic health are further propelling the market, positioning bioabsorbable interference screws as a vital component of future orthopedic practices.</p></p>
