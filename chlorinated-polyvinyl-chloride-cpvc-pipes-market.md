<p><p>Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride (CPVC) pipes are categorized into two primary market types based on their manufacturing processes: Extrusion PVC and Injection PVC. Extrusion PVC involves forcing molten CPVC through a die to create continuous lengths of pipe, ideal for plumbing and industrial applications, offering uniformity and strength. Injection PVC, on the other hand, molds CPVC into specific shapes or fittings by injecting it into a mold, allowing for complex designs and custom sizes. Both types cater to various construction and infrastructure needs.</p></p>
