<p><p>The peanut oil market is divided into two main types: refined and unrefined. Refined peanut oil undergoes extensive processing, removing impurities, flavor, and color, resulting in a light, neutral taste suitable for high-heat cooking and frying. Unrefined peanut oil retains its natural flavor, aroma, and nutrients, making it ideal for dressings and low-heat applications. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and culinary uses, with refined oil often favored for its versatility and unrefined oil for its rich flavor profile.</p></p>
